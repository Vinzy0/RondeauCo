*Nashville, Tennessee — Live-Fire American Grill*

### EARLY SPRING AT THE HEARTH

*Available Tuesday – Saturday through May 30, 2026*
*$185 per person · Wine pairing +$110*

Our seasonal tasting menu is a seven-course journey through the earliest offerings of the Tennessee spring — ramps emerging from the Smoky Mountain forest floor, the first asparagus from Sequatchie Valley, lamb born on bluegrass pasture, and strawberries still warm from the morning sun in White County.

Each course is built around a single defining ingredient at its absolute peak, prepared over our wood-burning hearth and presented with the precision and care that define Rondeau & Co. The accompanying wine pairing, curated by Sommelier David Paik, follows the arc of the menu from bright and mineral to deep and structured.

*The full tasting menu is served to the entire table. Please inform us of dietary restrictions at least 48 hours in advance so we may prepare thoughtful alternatives.*

---

### THE MENU

---

**I. AMUSE-BOUCHE**

**Ramp Butter on Warm Ash-Baked Flatbread**

Hand-foraged Appalachian ramps, churned into cultured butter with lemon zest and Maldon salt. Served on a small round of flatbread baked directly in the coals of our hearth. A single bite to awaken the palate and announce the season.

*Wine Pairing: Schramsberg Blanc de Blancs 2020 · Napa Valley, CA*
*Crisp green apple and brioche notes cut through the richness of the ramp butter, while fine bubbles cleanse and reset.*

---

**II. FIRST COURSE**

**Hamachi Crudo, Pickled Rhubarb, Green Strawberry**

Thinly sliced Japanese amberjack, draped over a pool of sorrel oil. Pickled rhubarb batons and shaved green strawberry bring tartness and crunch. Finished with a whisper of jalapeño and pink peppercorn.

*(GF, DF)*

*Wine Pairing: Domaine Huet Vouvray Sec "Le Haut-Lieu" 2021 · Loire Valley, France*
*The Chenin Blanc's honeyed minerality mirrors the crudo's delicate sweetness, while its acidity lifts the pickled rhubarb.*

---

**III. SECOND COURSE**

**Wood-Grilled Asparagus, Poached Farm Egg, Pecorino Fonduta**

Fat-stemmed Sequatchie Valley asparagus, charred whole over hickory coals until blistered and just tender. A slow-poached egg from Wedge Oak Farm breaks into a warm pecorino and black pepper fonduta. Topped with shaved black truffle and a scatter of fried breadcrumbs.

*(V, GF)*

*Wine Pairing: Planeta Etna Bianco 2021 · Sicily, Italy*
*Volcanic minerality and a saline undercurrent pair beautifully with the umami-rich fonduta and the smoke on the asparagus.*

---

**IV. THIRD COURSE**

**Ember-Roasted Vidalia Onion Soup**

Whole Vidalia onions, roasted for three hours in dying embers until deeply caramelized and nearly jammy. Deglazed with aged sherry and enriched with house-made veal stock. Served in a ceramic crock beneath a dome of blistered Gruyère and thyme-scented crouton.

*(V)*

*Wine Pairing: López de Heredia "Viña Tondonia" Blanco Reserva 2012 · Rioja, Spain*
*Oxidative, nutty, and hauntingly complex — this aged white Rioja meets the deep sweetness of ember-roasted Vidalia with an almost eerie harmony.*

---

**V. FOURTH COURSE — FISH**

**Smoked Trout, Spring Pea Purée, Country Ham Dashi**

Steelhead trout from a sustainable Appalachian hatchery, cured lightly and then cold-smoked over applewood. Set atop a vivid green purée of English peas and mint and surrounded by a delicate, umami-rich broth made from Benton's country ham and kombu.

*(GF)*

*Wine Pairing: Apriori Chardonnay 2021 · Sonoma Coast, CA*
*Bright citrus and restrained oak complement the smoky trout, while the wine's lush texture echoes the pea purée.*

---

**VI. FIFTH COURSE — MAIN**

**Grilled Spring Lamb Rack, Morel Mushrooms, Fava Bean Ragout**

A four-bone rack of Kentucky spring lamb, rubbed with dried za'atar and grilled over a combination of cherry and white oak until the exterior is deeply bronzed and the interior remains a perfect rosy medium-rare. Accompanied by a ragout of hand-shelled fava beans, foraged morel mushrooms, and spring garlic, all finished with a lamb jus reduced with fresh mint and a few drops of aged red wine vinegar.

*(GF)*

*Wine Pairing: Domaine Dujac Morey-Saint-Denis 1er Cru 2019 · Burgundy, France*
*Ethereal Pinot Noir with wild strawberry, earth, and spice — a classic pairing for lamb, elevated by the morel's forest-floor quality.*

---

**VII. DESSERT**

**White County Strawberries, Buttermilk Panna Cotta, Pistachio Crumble**

Peak-season strawberries from a single Tennessee farm, macerated lightly in aged balsamic and raw sugar. Cradled in a set buttermilk panna cotta scented with vanilla bean and lemon verbena. Finished with a crumble of roasted Sicilian pistachio and a drizzle of Tennessee wildflower honey.

*(V, GF)*

*Wine Pairing: Royal Tokaji "Gold Label" 5 Puttonyos 2017 · Tokaj, Hungary*
*Apricot, honey, and botrytis sweetness dance with the strawberries, while bracing acidity keeps the dessert from ever feeling heavy.*
