# RONDEAU & CO.
## Our Story

*Nashville, Tennessee — Live-Fire American Grill*

---

### THE NAME

In classical French cookery, a *rondeau* is a wide, heavy-bottomed vessel — low-sided and built for slow reduction, for layering flavors over time. It is not a flashy piece of equipment. It's the workhorse. The pan you reach for when the dish demands patience and technique over spectacle.

That's the spirit behind Rondeau & Co. — a restaurant built on the belief that the most extraordinary meals come from restraint, deep knowledge of fire, and an uncompromising respect for the ingredient. The "&nbsp;Co." speaks to the people: the farmers, the foragers, the ranchers, the sommeliers, and every hand that touches a plate before it reaches your table.

---

### THE BEGINNING

Rondeau & Co. opened its doors in the spring of 2019, occupying a restored 1920s brick warehouse on 3rd Avenue South in Nashville's SoBro district. The building — once a cold storage facility for the old Nashville produce market — was stripped back to its original timber beams, exposed brick, and poured concrete floors, then reimagined as a 72-seat dining room centered around an open wood-burning hearth.

The concept was born from a simple conviction: Nashville had earned its place on the national culinary stage, but there was room for a restaurant that treated live-fire cooking not as a trend, but as a discipline. One that merged the precision of classical technique with the primal, elemental power of cooking over wood and coal.

The project was led by chef Elias Marchetti and restaurateur Catherine Xu, who had spent years in New York and Chicago's fine dining circuits before relocating to Tennessee. Together, they envisioned a restaurant that would feel like a generational institution from its very first night — the kind of place that doesn't chase what's new, but instead commits to what endures.

### CHEF ELIAS MARCHETTI

**Executive Chef & Co-Founder**

Elias Marchetti grew up in Knoxville, the son of an Italian-American father who ran a small butcher shop and a mother from Oaxaca whose mole recipes were the stuff of neighborhood legend. Food, for Elias, was never abstract — it was the language his family spoke.

After graduating from the Culinary Institute of America in Hyde Park, Marchetti spent four years at Gramercy Tavern under Michael Anthony, where he developed his reverence for seasonal, market-driven cooking. He then moved to Chicago, where he served as sous chef at Avec and later as chef de cuisine at a Michelin-starred tasting menu restaurant on Randolph Street, earning recognition for his technical precision and his intuitive understanding of smoke, char, and open flame.

In 2018, Marchetti returned to Tennessee with a clear vision: to build a hearth-driven restaurant that honored Southern ingredients through the lens of classical training. At Rondeau & Co., his menus shift with the seasons, sourcing primarily from a network of Tennessee and Appalachian farms, pastures, and waterways. His cooking is direct, deliberate, and deeply personal — each plate reflecting years of accumulated knowledge and a relentless pursuit of flavor.

Marchetti was named a James Beard Award semifinalist for Best Chef: Southeast in 2023 and 2024.

### CATHERINE XU

**Co-Founder & Managing Partner**

Catherine Xu brings fifteen years of hospitality leadership to Rondeau & Co. A graduate of Cornell's School of Hotel Administration, she spent her early career in New York, managing front-of-house operations at Union Square Hospitality Group before joining the opening team of a celebrated Brooklyn wine bar.

Xu's philosophy is that fine dining should never feel exclusionary. At Rondeau & Co., she has built a service culture defined by warmth, knowledge, and genuine care — where a first-time guest feels as attended to as a regular. Her wine program, built in collaboration with sommelier David Paik, emphasizes small-production, terroir-driven bottles from both domestic and old-world estates.

### OUR PHILOSOPHY

**Fire is our kitchen.**
Our custom-built hearth — seven feet wide, fueled by Tennessee white oak, hickory, and cherry wood — is the centerpiece of everything we do. We don't use gas. Every protein, every vegetable, every crust of bread is touched by live flame, radiant heat, or smoldering coal. Fire is not a garnish here. It is the method.

**Ingredients dictate the menu.**
We don't write a menu and then source ingredients. We source first, then build around what's extraordinary. Our relationships with over two dozen farms, ranches, and foragers across Tennessee, Kentucky, and the broader Appalachian region mean our menu changes frequently — sometimes nightly — based on what's at peak.

**Hospitality is the other half.**
A great meal is as much about how you feel as what you eat. Our service team is trained to read the room, to know when to engage and when to disappear. We want you to linger. We want the last sip of wine to feel as considered as the first bite.

### THE SPACE

Rondeau & Co. seats 72 guests across the main dining room, an elevated chef's counter overlooking the hearth, and a candlelit lounge with a dedicated cocktail and raw bar. Two private dining rooms — The Hearth Room and The Cellar — accommodate intimate gatherings and larger celebrations.

The interior was designed by Nashville-based studio Corbin & Gray, drawing on the building's industrial heritage while introducing warmth through walnut millwork, hand-thrown ceramic tableware from East Tennessee potter Margot Sloane, and a curated collection of original works by Southern artists.
